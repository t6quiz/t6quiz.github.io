VT-6 NATOPS BRIEF

1) Aircrew

  1. EP / Question / Quote of the Day
  2. IMSAFE / Human Factors / Crew Day and Rest / Work Week limits
  3. Form Specific: Designated Section Lead / Formation Lead
  4. Seat Position (INAV or IP/IP only)
  5. Current read and initial
  6. Current NATOPS / Pubs / Charts/ EKB packed & charged
  7. SNA EKB use and restrictions — SNA should brief the acceptable use according to
    COMTRAWINGFIVEINST 3710.19F and enclosure (4) of same instruction.
  8. Review ATJ to include:
    1. Airsickness history
    2. Below MIF / incomplete items in block / SSRs
    3. Previous hop complete or incomplete
    4. Mandatory or optional warm up window
    5. Review green/blue card
  9. DOR/TTO Policy
  10. JPPT Discussion Items (May be moved to end of brief at IP’s discretion)

2) Mission Conduct

  1. Side Number / Callsign
  2. Walk / Takeoff / Land times
  3. Weather / NOTAMS / AHAS / TFRs
  4. TOLD (student calculates own TOLD data) For KNSE, plan worst case scenario: 6,000 ft runway, 0 winds, RWY14 at .7% downhill grade.
  5. Flight Plan
  6. Profile – Origin / Departure / Stage Maneuvers / Destination / Alternate
  7. Fuel management
    1. Joker / Bingo / Divert – Discuss reasoning for Joker / Bingo / Divert values and when to change them.
    2. Minimum / Emergency Fuel – Declare minimum or emergency fuel if calculated to land below 200lbs or 120lbs respectively.

  9. Radio procedures and discipline
  10. Change of aircraft controls
  11. Non flying pilot duties for IMC flight and instrument approaches
